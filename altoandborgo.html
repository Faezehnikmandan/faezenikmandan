Year : 2023-2024
Type : Professional Project
Location : Sansicario Torinese,Italy
Website : www.sunsicario.com , www.gasarchitettura.com

The project for the new Village Ski Hotel Resort CLUB MED in Sansicario is located in the municipality of Cesana Torinese, in the San Sicario Alto area, within the Via Lattea ski area. It includes a new hotel structure with 1,000 beds, a wellness center, a spa, swimming pools, and restaurants serving local specialties.

As an Architectural Assistant at Gas Architettura Studio, I was entrusted with a range of tasks that involved both technical drawing and visualization. My key responsibilities included the following:

  • Creating 2D Drawings: I was responsible for producing highly detailed 2D drawings using AutoCAD. These included executive-level detailed plans, elevations, and sections. Each drawing adhered to the precise requirements of the project, ensuring accuracy in measurements and specifications to guide construction teams and ensure compliance with architectural standards.
  • Developing 3D Models: I worked extensively with Revit to develop intricate 3D models. This process included crafting detailed floor plans, elevations, and sections within the model to provide a comprehensive representation of the building's design. The models were utilized for various purposes, including design presentations, technical assessments, and coordination with other project teams.
  • Rendering Interior and Exterior Views: Using Enscape, I generated photorealistic renderings of both the interior and exterior spaces of projects. These renders were essential for client presentations, providing a clear and visually compelling view of the proposed designs. I ensured that the materials, lighting, and textures accurately reflected the intended design vision.
  • Producing Isometric Plans: I also created isometric plans to offer a three-dimensional perspective of the floor layouts, enhancing the understanding of spatial relationships within the design. This required an eye for detail and an ability to present complex spatial arrangements in an easily interpretable format.
  • Incorporating Proposed Materials with Photoshop: To finalize and present the proposed designs, I used Photoshop to edit and enhance images by incorporating the suggested materials, finishes, and textures. This was a crucial step in producing polished visual presentations, highlighting the intended aesthetic and functional elements of the design.

Through these responsibilities, I gained a comprehensive understanding of the architectural design process, from initial concepts to detailed visual presentations. This experience also sharpened my technical skills and broadened my knowledge of architectural software and techniques.
